## Prolegomena

Across the world, events are happening that do not fit neatly within the limits of ordinary science. They whisper of something more—an intelligence, a purpose, a will moving through them. And reason tells us something simple but profound: when we see intelligent effects, we must look for an intelligent cause.

Through careful observation and humble inquiry, something remarkable has been learned: this intelligence can communicate with us. It can answer. It can respond. It can speak through physical means we can observe. And when asked to identify itself, this presence declares that it belongs to the realm of spirit—the realm of souls who have left behind the physical body.

This is the origin of what we now call the Doctrine of the Spirits.

These communications are not supernatural. They belong to the deep fabric of nature itself. Human history is filled with traces of these same encounters—occurring in every culture, in every age. What is different today is not the phenomenon, but the clarity with which it appears. The veil has thinned. What was once rare and scattered has become widespread and unmistakable.

The Spirits say the time has arrived. Providence has opened a new chapter in the story of humanity. As servants of the Divine, they come with a mission: to teach, to illuminate, and to help lift the human family into a more conscious, compassionate era.

This book gathers their teachings.

It was composed at the instruction—and under the guidance—of spirits of higher wisdom. The ideas do not belong to one human mind; they were offered, shaped, corrected, and confirmed by the spirits themselves. The arrangement and the connective commentary belong to the one given the responsibility to publish the work, but the substance is theirs.

Not all who contributed carry names remembered by history. Some lived on earth and taught with virtue and clarity in their time. Others remain unnamed but reveal their elevated nature through the purity of their message and the harmony they share with those of honored memory.

What follows are the instructions given by the spirits themselves through multiple mediums—words that mark the mission entrusted to those who prepared this work:

---

### **“Be faithful to the task entrusted to you…”**

“Be diligent and steadfast in this work you are undertaking with us, for this endeavor belongs to us as much as to you. Together, we are laying the foundations of a new way of seeing—an edifice meant to gather all humankind into a spirit of love and goodwill.

“Before this book is published, we will review it with you and ensure its accuracy.

“We will be with you whenever you call on us, guiding you through the rest of your mission, for this book is only one part of what we ask you to accomplish.

“Some teachings will be given to you only for your personal preparation. When the time comes, we will tell you to share them. Until then, meditate on them so that your heart will be ready.

“At the beginning of the book, place the vine branch we have drawn. It is the symbol of the Creator’s work. The stem represents the body; the sap, the spirit; the cluster of grapes, the soul joined to the body. Just as the vine grows through tending and labor, the spirit grows through effort in earthly life.

“Do not be disheartened by criticism. You will face resistance, especially from those who profit from falsehood. Some spirits, still burdened by selfishness, will try to sow doubt as well. Continue forward. Trust in God. We will uphold you. The time is near when truth will shine openly.

“Human pride will cause disagreements. Some will declare they know everything and insist on framing the Doctrine according to their own ideas. But those who truly follow the great principle of Jesus—the principle of love—will find unity. They will set aside disputes over words and focus on what matters most. Wherever high spirits communicate, the essential teaching will remain the same.

“Through perseverance, you will see the fruit of your labor. The joy of watching the Doctrine spread and be understood will be its own reward. Perhaps its full value will be known only in the future.

“Do not fear the stones that skeptics may cast along your path. Stay confident, and you will reach the goal. Our help will always be with you.

“Remember: good spirits aid only those who serve God with humility and without self-interest. They withdraw from the proud, the ambitious, and those who turn heavenly things into earthly gain. Pride blinds the eyes of the soul. Ambition thickens the veil. And God does not use the blind to explain the light.”

— St. John the Evangelist, St. Augustine, St. Vincent de Paul, St. Louis, the Spirit of Truth, Socrates, Plato, Fénelon, Franklin, Swedenborg, and others
